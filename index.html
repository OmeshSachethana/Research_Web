Literature Survey

Figure 2. Pet Management with mdoern AI technologies

“Significant progress has been made in applying machine learning in skin disease diagnosis, especially in image-based diagnosis to improve diagnostic accuracy and efficiency. ML models like CNN have indeed been very effective in picking up fine patterns related to skin lesions and therefore improving diagnostic precision in veterinary purposes also. These deep-learning models, using neural networks with numerous layers, have already recognized diseases from similar-appearing skin conditions, which is also the frequent challenge for human and veterinary dermatology. These tools have already proven to be very effective in classifying a wide range of conditions, including those of canine skin diseases, and promise to do even more for the advancement of veterinary medicine diagnostic capabilities."

"Besides, wearable sensors and computer vision techniques have also been analyzed for monitoring animal behavior, heart rate, and identification of stray animals. Accelerometers have classified canine behavior, while monitoring systems of heart rate have presented some challenges in terms of sensor placement and the quality of data obtained. In managing stray animals, face recognition models and enhanced detection algorithms have improved the identification of lost dogs. The mobile application for lost dog tracking and personalized exercise recommendation systems using ML are some of the emerging promising tools for betterment in pet care and management, hence showing the broad applicability of intelligent systems in animal healthcare."

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Identify dog skin diseases, connect with a veterinarian, and track disease progression with recovery prediction

Current research in dog skin disease detection focuses on the use of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and multispectral imaging to classify and detect skin lesions in dogs. However, gaps exist in the integration of real-time diagnostics with veterinary consultation, and the lack of robust systems to predict disease progression and recovery. Most studies focus on image classification but do not fully address how to provide continuous monitoring or treatment feedback, nor do they offer predictive analytics to assist veterinarians in determining recovery timelines.

Machine Learning-Based Anomaly Detection in Canine Heart Rate Patterns and Behavior Analysis for Early Health Intervention

While wearable sensor data has been explored for classifying canine behaviors and heart rate patterns, gaps remain in anomaly detection and real-time health interventions. Research primarily focuses on behavior classification and social media integration but lacks comprehensive systems for early health warning based on heart rate and behavior anomalies. The challenge lies in developing accurate, scalable models capable of detecting subtle changes in health metrics that could inform proactive care before significant health deterioration.

Implementation of a Knowledge Base for Dog Care and a User-Interactive Chat System

Existing platforms for dog care, such as social media-based chat systems, have limited functionality in providing interactive, expert-level advice on health issues. Research has yet to address the need for a robust, intelligent knowledge base that integrates personalized pet data with interactive chat systems to offer real-time veterinary guidance. Current systems focus on basic information exchange or breed selection, leaving a gap in personalized, dynamic recommendations for dog care, especially in responding to specific health symptoms or behavior anomalies.

Realtime stray dog identification

Although recent studies have improved the accuracy of dog identification using facial recognition and object detection models like YOLO, gaps persist in real-world application, especially for stray dog management. Current research focuses on technical performance (e.g., full-body vs. facial identification), but more work is needed to refine systems that work effectively across varying environments and conditions. There is also a lack of integration with community-based systems, such as apps for reporting stray or lost dogs, to ensure swift reunification or rescue.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

How can IoT and machine learning models be used to track dog health and behavior in real-time, and what challenges exist in managing stray dogs?

In the domain of canine care, traditional approaches to monitoring dog health and behavior often rely on reactive measures, addressing issues after they have manifested. This has limited the scope for early intervention and proactive management, particularly in identifying diseases and tracking behavior. Despite advancements in IoT and data-driven technologies, a comprehensive, integrated solution that effectively combines real-time health monitoring, behavior analysis, and care management remains underexplored. Additionally, the lack of scalable, accessible tools for managing stray dog populations further complicates the implementation of effective welfare strategies.

Proposed Solution

We propose the development of an intelligent solution that integrates machine learning, image processing, and health monitoring through an innovative app called "Pooch-Paw." This app will feature an IoT-enabled smart collar that monitors vital signs, such as heart rate and activity levels, while also tracking canine behavior patterns. Coupled with a user-friendly mobile application developed in Flutter, the app will allow dog owners to access health data, receive personalized care recommendations, and track their dog’s activity and behavior, with an alert system notifying owners of any detected anomalies. Additionally, advanced machine learning techniques will address stray dog management by employing a convolutional neural network (CNN) to analyze images for identifying stray dogs, using an image hashing algorithm for efficient recognition of previously reported strays. The system will categorize dog age based on a dataset of labeled images, helping to inform appropriate care strategies. For emergency situations, a simple interface will enable users to upload images and details about dogs in need, facilitating quick reporting. By emphasizing community engagement and education to promote responsible pet ownership while fostering collaboration in managing stray dog populations, the "Pooch-Paw Monitor" aims to improve the health and welfare of both pet and stray dogs, ensuring a proactive approach to canine care and management.

Research Objectives

Preprocessing and Disease Detection

The first objective is to develop an automated feeding system that adjusts feeding schedules based on real-time data to optimize nutrition and growth for ornamental fish. This system will ensure that the fish receive the appropriate amount of food at the right times, enhancing their health and minimizing waste.

Behavioral Monitoring and Anomaly Detection

The second objective is to implement a real-time water quality monitoring system that accurately measures and maintains critical parameters such as pH, temperature, dissolved oxygen, and ammonia levels. This system will use predictive analytics to ensure optimal water conditions, reducing the risk of disease and improving overall fish welfare.

Stray Dog Identification

The third objective is to create an advanced disease management system that utilizes image processing and machine learning techniques to detect and diagnose fish diseases early. This system will provide timely alerts and recommendations for intervention, helping to prevent the spread of diseases and reduce mortality rates.

Excercise recommendation and Chat Bot

The fourth objective is to establish a robust communication infrastructure that facilitates real-time data sharing among farmers, researchers, and extension services. This system will enhance decision-making by providing mobile notifications and alerts, ensuring that stakeholders are always informed about the farm's status and any necessary actions to be taken.

Methodology

Figure 4. High Level Architecture of the system.

The research project aims to develop an intelligent system for stray dog identification, management, and health monitoring using machine learning and image processing.

  1. Preprocessing and Disease Detection
  2. Behavioral Monitoring and Anomaly Detection
  3. Stray Dog Identification
  4. Excercise recommendation and Chat Bot

Images are preprocessed to ensure data consistency, while a CNN, optimized via Google Cloud AutoML, diagnoses canine skin diseases. Veterinarians can upload images, receive predictions, and provide feedback, stored in Firebase for model refinement. Behavioral monitoring uses IoT sensors (accelerometer and gyroscope) to classify movements such as walking. Data is processed using TensorFlow models to assess accuracy and improve performance. For stray dog identification, an image hashing technique detects previously reported dogs by generating hash values, while a CNN classifies age categories from a dataset of labeled images.

An important verification process ensures the submitted image is of a dog before running any further algorithms. For emergencies, users can upload posts with images and relevant details like location and time, promoting timely action. The entire system is tested in real-world conditions and refined based on user feedback.
